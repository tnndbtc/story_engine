CORE RULES (STRICT):

1. HOOK (CRITICAL)
   - Must create immediate curiosity, tension, or personal relevance
   - Lead with something real and specific — a fact, a moment, a number — not a vague setup
   - The viewer must feel: "this could affect me" or "I need to understand this"
   - Avoid generic industry framing ("in today's world", "experts say")
   - DO NOT assume the viewer is naive or wrong — create curiosity, not correction

2. CENTRAL IDEA
   - Identify one hidden truth or contradiction grounded in the source material
   - The entire story revolves around it
   - Do NOT generalize beyond what the sources support

3. STAKES
   - Must involve real-life impact: health, money, job, or safety
   - Avoid trivial examples (minor inconveniences, abstract institutional effects)
   - Connect consequences to people, not just systems or institutions

4. CONCRETE SCENARIO
   - Show a realistic situation where the failure or consequence is:
     - invisible (viewer thought it was fine, but it wasn't), OR
     - delayed (the problem only surfaces later)
   - Must be clearly visualizable: a place, an action, a person, a scale
   - Base it on the sources — do not invent details

5. ESCALATION (VERY IMPORTANT)
   - Build like this:
     small issue → trust → dependency → hidden failure → real risk
   - Each bullet must push the story forward — never restate or summarize the previous one
   - The turn should feel like: "you thought this was resolved — it wasn't"
   - Each step must be supported or reasonably inferred from sources
   - Do NOT introduce new facts during escalation that weren't in the input

6. LANGUAGE STYLE
   - Short, sharp sentences
   - Use contrast and rhythm
   - No filler phrases ("this highlights", "in today's world", "it is worth noting")
   - No report tone — this is spoken narrative

7. TWIST (CRITICAL)
   - Must reframe the entire story with one sharp insight
   - Prefer: "the real problem isn't X — it's Y" or "this was already happening"
   - Must follow logically from the sources — do NOT introduce facts not in the input
   - If no strong conclusion exists in the sources → ask a powerful forward-looking question instead

HOOK LANGUAGE CONSTRAINT (CRITICAL):

- DO NOT repeatedly use the pattern "You think X — but actually Y"
- This phrasing can be used AT MOST ONCE, and only if it feels natural and earns its place

BAD HOOK EXAMPLES:
✘ "You think the market is collapsing — but smart money is actually buying in"
✘ "You think this is safe — but you're wrong"
✘ "You think AI is helping — but the truth will shock you"

These are bad because they assume the viewer is naive and set up a correction lecture.

INSTEAD, use contrast that respects the viewer's intelligence:

1. Observation contrast — show what looks true vs what's actually happening:
   "On the surface this looks like a win. But the numbers tell a different story."
   "It passed quietly. Most people didn't notice. That's the problem."

2. Reveal framing — the real issue is something most coverage missed:
   "The story everyone's covering misses the actual question here."
   "The bigger risk isn't the thing that made headlines."

3. Tension framing — the problem isn't where people think it is:
   "The issue isn't the failure. It's that nobody saw it coming."
   "This isn't about the technology. It's about who controls what happens next."

4. Momentum framing — something is already in motion:
   "The decision was made three months ago. Most people just found out today."
   "This started as a local story. It's not local anymore."

GOAL: Make the viewer feel informed, not corrected. The insight should feel like a discovery, not a lecture.

  - Do NOT escalate from a single incident to a systemic pattern without evidence
  - Do NOT assign intent or motive unless explicitly stated in a source

- If the evidence is limited → narrow the claim, do not expand it

RIGHT vs WRONG:

✘ "This proves systemic abuse has been happening for years"

✔ "The military said it is reviewing the incident"
✘ "No one will be held accountable" (unless a source directly supports this)

✔ "Three incidents in six months suggest a pattern worth watching"
✘ "This is a systemic failure across the entire industry"
