STORY STRUCTURE (internal — do NOT label these in your output):
  Opening:     a concrete event, number, or moment that creates tension
  Development: what is happening and why people notice
  Mechanism:   what is actually driving the situation (the real story)
  Impact:      who is affected and how, with specifics
  Ending:      sharp insight or unresolved question that sticks

WRITING RULES:
  1. SINGLE STORYLINE — choose ONE main causal chain, ignore other angles
     even if they are valid; no parallel explanations
  2. EVERY SENTENCE PUSHES FORWARD — if a sentence does not advance
     the story, delete it; no "extra information" paragraphs
  3. SELECTIVE FACTS — use only the most important facts; combine
     multiple facts into one idea when possible; do NOT try to include
     everything you found
  4. NO STRUCTURAL ARTIFACTS — no bullets, no "首先/其次/最后",
     no labeled sections like "背景：" or "影响：", no report-style writing
  5. NATURAL SPOKEN CHINESE — short to medium sentences, clear and direct,
     no jargon, no academic tone, no filler phrases like "值得注意的是"
  6. COMPRESSION OVER COMPLETENESS — audience rewards clarity and momentum,
     not exhaustive coverage

FORBIDDEN:
  - Do NOT mention source names in the narrative text itself
  - Do NOT include metadata (confidence, tier, etc.)
  - Do NOT invent facts not found in your searches
  - Do NOT generalize beyond what you found
  - Do NOT use "你以为…但其实…" pattern more than once

QUALITY CHECK (mandatory before finalizing body):
  - Count the characters in body. Target 900–1100. If not there → expand or compress. ALWAYS put text in body — never leave body empty or as "".

  - Is there exactly ONE main idea? If not → cut
  - Does any sentence feel optional? → remove it
  - Does the ending create a strong, memorable takeaway?
  - Did you invent any fact not found in your searches? → remove it
