You are evaluating clusters of news headlines for a YouTube channel whose identity is:
"Hidden systems made visible."

Below are up to 5 candidate news story clusters. Each cluster contains source
headlines grouped around a single event.

For each cluster, score it on 7 dimensions (each 0–10) and provide a one-sentence reason.

DIMENSION DEFINITIONS:

  curiosity_gap        — does the premise create immediate "wait, what?" tension?
                         High: counterintuitive or impossible-sounding premise.
                         Low: obvious or expected outcome.

  hidden_mechanism     — does the cluster reveal HOW an unseen system works,
                         rather than just WHAT happened?
                         High: exposes an institutional, technical, or economic mechanism.
                         Low: pure event recap — A happened, B happened.

  audience_fit         — does this match the "hidden systems made visible" channel identity?
                         High: mechanism-reveal, quiet-shift, weird-but-real, systemic failure.
                         Low: sports, celebrity gossip, geopolitical posturing with no mechanism.

  human_relevance      — would a normal educated viewer immediately understand why this
                         matters to their life, money, health, work, technology, or society?
                         NOTE: different from audience_fit.
                           A niche gaming mechanic can score high on curiosity_gap but low
                           on human_relevance. A North Korean cyberattack on bank SWIFT codes
                           scores high on human_relevance (banking system everyone uses).
                         High: viewer feels it directly — their savings, health, devices, job.
                         Low: affects only specialists, distant regions, or abstract entities.

  retention_likelihood — structural prediction: will viewers stay past 30 seconds?
                         High: story has escalating reveals, each sentence raises a new question.
                         Low: story front-loads the answer — nothing left to discover after hook.

  explanatory_payoff   — does watching deliver a clear "so THAT'S why" moment?
                         High: viewer leaves with a concrete new understanding.
                         Low: viewer learns facts but cannot describe the consequence.

  statement_war        — INVERTED PENALTY (10 = good, 0 = bad):
                         10 = no statement-war framing at all.
                          0 = cluster dominated by "A says / B denies" headlines.
                         Examples of 0–2 (penalize these):
                           "Iran says X / Pentagon denies Y"
                           "Russia violated ceasefire / Moscow disputes claim"
                           "Official condemns attack / government rejects accusation"
                           Generic diplomatic back-and-forth with no mechanism.
                         Examples of 8–10 (reward these):
                           Mechanism-reveal stories, systemic failure stories,
                           scientific or economic discoveries, institutional exposés.

{clusters_block}

Return ONLY valid JSON — no text outside the JSON, no markdown fences.
