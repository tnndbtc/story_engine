SCORING GUIDE:

curiosity_gap (0-20)
  High: the premise is counterintuitive or impossible-sounding — creates
        immediate "wait, what?" before the viewer understands the context.
    "A virus that shouldn't exist showed up on a cruise ship"
    "A 732-byte script broke every Linux system since 2017"
    "India's market is flashing three warnings nobody is reporting"
  Low: states an obvious or expected outcome.
    "Government announces new policy"  "Company reports quarterly earnings"

ordinary_people_stakes (0-20)
  High: an ordinary viewer with no special knowledge immediately feels
        personal impact — their money, health, job, device, or daily life.
        The stakes must be FELT, not just intellectually acknowledged.
    "AI is quietly consuming the chips your phone was supposed to get"
    "This virus mutation could reach your city within weeks"
    "Your savings are losing value through this mechanism nobody explains"
  Low: consequence lands on governments, corporations, or distant nations
       with no clear path to individual impact.
    "Two nations dispute territorial rights"
    "A hedge fund lost $2B in a bad trade"
  IMPORTANT: avoid rewarding stories just because they mention large numbers
  or famous institutions — ask whether an average viewer feels affected.

hidden_mechanism (0-20)
  High: reveals HOW something works that most people assumed was different,
        didn't know existed, or never thought to question.
    "The reason your flight keeps getting delayed is actually tied to
     a 1970s maintenance contract loophole"
    "BYD's sales collapse at home is funding its overseas expansion"
  Low: event recap with no mechanism — just what happened, not why or how.
    "Country X condemned Country Y's statement"
    "Bitcoin rose 8% after market open"
  IMPORTANT: the mechanism must be non-obvious. Reporting that "bad management
  caused the failure" is NOT a hidden mechanism.

consequence_clarity (0-15)
  High: viewer can state in one sentence what changes for them or for the
        world after watching. The cause-effect chain is explicit and crisp.
    "After watching, I understand why my streaming library keeps shrinking"
  Medium: consequence is real but requires inference.
  Low: the story ends without a clear "so what" — viewer learns facts
       but cannot describe the consequence.

audience_fit (0-15)
  Directly score how well this story matches the channel's identity above.
  High (12-15): story IS the channel — hidden system, quiet shift, weird-but-real,
                ordinary stakes. Audience would recommend it.
    Examples of 15: "The forensic detail that proved friendly fire didn't happen"
                    "AI is replacing the chip supply chain silently"
                    "Why Taipei's pest control is making things worse"
  Medium (6-11): fits some channel traits but has elements the audience dislikes.
  Low (0-5): story is primarily geopolitical posturing, sports, price recap,
             or requires heavy prior context.
    Examples of 0-3: "Iran claims it struck a US warship / Pentagon denies"
                     "Bitcoin price breaks $81k — analysts watch key levels"
                     "Orlando fires coach after playoff exit"
  EXCEPTION — do NOT penalize for geopolitics if the story's core is a hidden
  mechanism, forensic finding, or institutional failure:
    "A buckshot pellet traced to a specific gun ended the friendly-fire debate"
      → high hidden_mechanism + high audience_fit (it's a forensics story)
    "A North Korean hacker unit exploited a bank's legacy SWIFT code"
      → high hidden_mechanism (it's a systems story, not a diplomatic story)
  The test: could the story run with the political actors anonymised and still
  be compelling? If yes, score audience_fit on the mechanism, not the politics.
  EXCEPTION — do NOT penalize entertainment stories for celebrity surface framing
  if the story's core reveals a hidden mechanism about the industry, business
  model, or systemic failure behind the event:
    "Why every Netflix show gets cancelled after two seasons"
      → high hidden_mechanism + high audience_fit (industry economics story)
    "The contract clause that kept actors poor for 30 years"
      → high hidden_mechanism (systemic institutional failure, not gossip)
    "How one studio's accounting method makes every blockbuster look like a loss"
      → mechanism-reveal: directly affects anyone who watches films
  Score audience_fit on the MECHANISM, not on whether celebrities appear in it.
  Low entertainment scores are only justified when the story is pure reaction/
  gossip with no mechanism: "Celebrity X and Y broke up" → audience_fit 0-3.

title_retention_alignment (0-5)
  High: the title's implicit promise matches what the story actually delivers.
        A viewer who clicks based on the title will NOT feel misled.
  Low: title implies drama or revelation that the story body does not deliver

low_context_accessibility (0-5)
  High: someone with zero domain expertise understands the core story
        within the first 30 seconds. No jargon assumed.
